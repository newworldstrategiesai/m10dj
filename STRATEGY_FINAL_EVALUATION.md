### 2. **Pricing Strategy - Smart Restraint**

**What They Got Right:**
- ✅ Keep transaction fees as-is (they're competitive)
- ✅ Annual discounts (20-28%) are standard and effective
- ✅ Add-ons later, not day 1 (reduces complexity)
- ✅ No per-event pricing initially (avoids billing complexity)

**Why This Works:**
- Predictable revenue for DJs (they prefer this)
- Transaction fees justify themselves with value
- Add-ons can be tested after PMF is proven

**One Addition to Consider:**
- **Volume discounts on transaction fees**: If a DJ does $5K+/month in transactions, drop to 2% + $0.10
- This rewards high-volume users and creates stickiness

**The Problem:**

- Signups spike March-May
- Churn attempts spike Jan-Feb

**The Solutions:**
- 2 months free for annual in Nov/Dec
- "Off-season reset" campaign in January
- Multi-year contracts with bigger discounts

**Why This Works:**
- Addresses real business problem
- Incentivizes annual contracts (smooths revenue)
- Helps DJs during slow season (builds loyalty)

**One Addition:**
- **Off-season features**: Launch new features in Jan-Feb when DJs have time to learn
- **Content marketing**: "How to book more events in off-season" guides
- **Community events**: Virtual meetups during slow season

- **Referral**: They refer DJs, DJ signs up, becomes paid customer
- **Tracking**: Unique referral code or link
- **Payout**: $100-200 bounty when DJ becomes paid
- **Materials**: One-pager, email template, social media posts
- **Metrics**: Referrals sent, conversion rate, LTV of referred DJs
